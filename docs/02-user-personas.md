# 02 — User Personas

---

## Overview

Twogether serves couples at a specific, emotionally charged life stage: the period between engagement and marriage. Personas are defined not just by demographics but by **financial behavior**, **relationship dynamics**, and **emotional state**. Understanding these nuances is essential to designing features that feel relevant rather than generic.

---

## Persona 01: The Organized Planners

**Names:** Reza & Dinda  
**Ages:** 28 & 27  
**Location:** Jakarta, Indonesia  
**Occupation:** Marketing Manager & Graphic Designer  
**Engagement Duration:** 6 months  
**Wedding Timeline:** 12 months away  
**Tech Comfort:** High

### Profile
Reza and Dinda are the couple everyone envies. They have a shared Google Sheet for wedding expenses, a pinboard of venue ideas, and a detailed timeline. But their system is fragmented — spreadsheets for budgets, WhatsApp for decisions, and separate banking apps for savings. They want a unified place that feels as organized as they are, but less manual.

### Goals
- Consolidate all wedding planning and finance in one place
- Track who paid for what without awkward conversations
- See overall savings progress at a glance
- Have a checklist that both of them can update in real time

### Frustrations
- Google Sheets gets messy as it grows
- Hard to see "big picture" when data is in multiple places
- Partner sometimes forgets to update the shared document
- No way to visualize progress toward specific goals (venue, honeymoon, rings)

### Motivations
- Being prepared makes them feel secure about the future
- They want their wedding to reflect their values: thoughtful and intentional
- Financial transparency builds trust between them

### Behaviors
- Logs expenses frequently (multiple times per week)
- Reviews dashboards regularly
- Will customize categories and goals
- Will use the checklist heavily

### How Twogether Helps
Full feature engagement. This persona drives feature depth demand. They will find bugs, request improvements, and become power users and advocates.

## Persona 02: The Long-Distance Couple

**Names:** Arif & Sella  
**Ages:** 30 & 29  
**Location:** Surabaya (Arif) & Amsterdam (Sella)  
**Occupation:** Civil Engineer & PhD Student  
**Engagement Duration:** 3 months  
**Wedding Timeline:** 18 months away  
**Tech Comfort:** High

### Profile
Arif and Sella are separated by continents and time zones. Their wedding planning happens across video calls and messaging apps. Financial coordination is especially hard — they earn in different currencies, have different living costs, and struggle to have a shared financial picture.

### Goals
- Have a single source of truth for wedding finances across locations
- Understand combined savings despite different currencies (initially manual)
- Coordinate wedding tasks without being in the same room
- Feel emotionally connected to the wedding planning process despite the distance

### Frustrations
- Time zone differences mean decisions get delayed
- Easy to lose track of who contributed what
- Hard to feel like "we're planning together" when apart
- Different cost-of-living makes fair contributions hard to calculate

### Motivations
- Closing the distance — even through a shared app — feels meaningful
- Having structure reduces anxiety about the future
- Wants the wedding planning process to be something they share, not just manage

### Behaviors
- Logs contributions less frequently but in larger amounts
- Checks app independently at different times of day
- Will rely heavily on notifications and async features
- Will use the countdown emotionally, not just functionally

### How Twogether Helps
The shared dashboard and real-time updates make them feel synchronized even when apart. The countdown is particularly meaningful. Multi-currency support is a V1.5 priority driven by this persona.

## Persona 03: The Newly Engaged, Overwhelmed Couple

**Names:** Bagas & Putri  
**Ages:** 25 & 24  
**Location:** Bandung, Indonesia  
**Occupation:** Software Developer & Nurse  
**Engagement Duration:** 2 weeks  
**Wedding Timeline:** Uncertain (12–24 months)  
**Tech Comfort:** Medium–High

### Profile
Bagas and Putri just got engaged last month. They're excited but completely overwhelmed. Neither of them has planned a wedding before. They don't know where to start, how much things cost, or how to divide responsibilities. They need a guiding hand, not just a tool.

### Goals
- Understand what wedding planning even involves
- Start saving without knowing the exact target yet
- Have a structured checklist to follow
- Not fight about money or responsibilities

### Frustrations
- Decision paralysis: too many choices, no framework
- Afraid of making expensive mistakes
- Partner has very different assumptions about the wedding scale and budget
- No financial habits as a couple yet

### Motivations
- Excitement about getting married overcomes the anxiety — for now
- They want to do this "right" but don't know what that means
- Finishing tasks feels like progress and brings relief

### Behaviors
- Will rely heavily on the default checklist
- Will set vague savings goals and refine over time
- Will use the app inconsistently at first
- Needs encouraging copy and empty states that guide rather than overwhelm

### How Twogether Helps
Default checklist template is their lifeline. Onboarding flow that doesn't require knowing the wedding budget up front. Progress celebrations that reward early engagement even when the picture isn't complete.

## Persona 04: The Financially Disciplined Couple

**Names:** Hendra & Maya  
**Ages:** 32 & 31  
**Location:** Surabaya, Indonesia  
**Occupation:** Financial Analyst & Accountant  
**Engagement Duration:** 12 months  
**Wedding Timeline:** 6 months away  
**Tech Comfort:** High

### Profile
Hendra and Maya are the most financially sophisticated couple on this list. They already track personal expenses separately, have investment portfolios, and know their monthly cash flow to the rupiah. They are skeptical of apps that oversimplify. They want precision, not just warm design.

### Goals
- Accurately track who paid what and who owes whom
- See detailed breakdowns by category and time period
- Track savings contributions with precision
- Ensure their wedding budget doesn't exceed what they've planned

### Frustrations
- Apps that round numbers or don't show full transaction history
- Lack of export/reporting features
- Oversimplified split calculations
- No way to set custom budget limits per expense category

### Motivations
- Control and accuracy reduce anxiety
- They have high standards and won't use a product that feels "lite"
- Their discipline is a shared value and the app should respect it

### Behaviors
- Will push the limits of every feature
- Will check balance calculations manually
- Will request CSV exports and detailed reports
- May switch to spreadsheets if the app doesn't meet precision needs

### How Twogether Helps
Detailed transaction history, accurate balance calculations, and custom split ratios satisfy this persona. Reports and export features are V1.5 priorities influenced by them. Risk: they may find the product too simple — the app must earn their trust with accuracy.

## Persona 05: The Financially Struggling Couple

**Names:** Doni & Rani  
**Ages:** 26 & 25  
**Location:** Malang, Indonesia  
**Occupation:** Freelance Photographer & Elementary School Teacher  
**Engagement Duration:** 8 months  
**Wedding Timeline:** 14 months away  
**Tech Comfort:** Medium  
**Income:** Irregular (Doni) / Fixed but limited (Rani)

### Profile
Doni and Rani want to have a beautiful wedding but have real financial constraints. Doni's income is inconsistent. Rani's salary is fixed but small. They're also supporting family members financially. Their wedding budget is tight and every rupiah matters.

### Goals
- Save consistently even when income is irregular
- Understand where money is going and where it can be cut
- Feel hopeful, not ashamed, about their financial situation
- Plan a wedding that's within their means without feeling like they're settling

### Frustrations
- Feeling behind compared to friends with bigger budgets
- Shame or tension when discussing money
- Inconsistent ability to contribute to savings
- Lack of tools that support variable income

### Motivations
- Love and commitment motivate saving even when hard
- Small wins (hitting a milestone) are emotionally important
- Feeling like they're making progress together reduces tension

### Behaviors
- Contribution amounts vary month to month
- May go weeks without logging anything, then catch up
- Responds strongly to encouragement and milestone celebrations
- Needs the product to feel safe, non-judgmental, and supportive

### How Twogether Helps
No minimum contribution requirements. Progress is celebrated regardless of amount. Copy tone is encouraging, never implying failure for small balances. The product should never make this couple feel inadequate — that would be a design failure.

## Design Implications

1. **Onboarding must accommodate all these personas** — from those with no budget defined to those with precise figures ready.
2. **Copy tone must be universally warm and non-judgmental** — the financially struggling couple can't feel lesser.
3. **Feature depth must scale** — simple on the surface, powerful when you dig in.
4. **Async-first design** — long-distance couples remind us both partners won't always be online together.
5. **Celebrate small wins** — milestone animations and encouraging messages matter enormously to most of these personas.
